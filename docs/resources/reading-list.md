# Reading List: Foundations of Systematic Adversarial Methodology

This curated bibliography organizes 153 essential books across the domains foundational to S.A.M. methodology: philosophy, epistemology, cognitive science, institutional theory, legal analysis, research methodology, and related fields.

---

## Philosophy and Critical Thinking

### Logic and Argumentation

1. **Toulmin, Stephen E.** *The Uses of Argument* (1958)
   - Foundational text on argument structure and warrant-based reasoning
   - Essential for understanding claim-evidence relationships in S.A.M.

2. **Walton, Douglas.** *Argumentation Schemes for Presumptive Reasoning* (1996)
   - Formal schemas for analyzing arguments
   - Direct application to EVIDENTIARY contradiction detection

3. **Walton, Douglas.** *Informal Logic: A Pragmatic Approach* (2008)
   - Practical approach to argument analysis
   - Useful for identifying fallacies in institutional documents

4. **Copi, Irving M. & Cohen, Carl.** *Introduction to Logic* (14th ed., 2014)
   - Comprehensive logic foundation
   - Necessary background for formal contradiction taxonomy

5. **Fisher, Alec.** *The Logic of Real Arguments* (2004)
   - Analyzing arguments as they appear in real contexts
   - Directly applicable to document analysis

### Epistemology

6. **Goldman, Alvin I.** *Knowledge in a Social World* (1999)
   - Social epistemology foundations
   - How social institutions produce and validate knowledge

7. **Kitcher, Philip.** *Science, Truth, and Democracy* (2001)
   - Relationship between knowledge production and institutional power
   - Relevant to understanding authority laundering

8. **Harman, Gilbert.** *Change in View: Principles of Reasoning* (1986)
   - How beliefs should be revised in light of new evidence
   - Application to tracking certainty shifts

9. **Williamson, Timothy.** *Knowledge and Its Limits* (2000)
   - Epistemology of uncertainty
   - Understanding limits of institutional knowledge claims

10. **Sosa, Ernest.** *A Virtue Epistemology: Apt Belief and Reflective Knowledge* (2007)
    - Virtue epistemology framework
    - Standards for epistemic responsibility in institutional settings

### Philosophy of Science

11. **Kuhn, Thomas S.** *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* (1962)
    - Paradigm shifts and resistance to evidence
    - Analogous to institutional resistance to correction

12. **Popper, Karl R.** *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (1959)
    - Falsificationism and critical testing
    - Adversarial methodology foundations

13. **Lakatos, Imre.** *The Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes* (1978)
    - How research programs evolve and degenerate
    - Applicable to institutional claim evolution

14. **Feyerabend, Paul.** *Against Method* (1975)
    - Critique of methodological rigidity
    - Useful counterpoint: importance of methodological flexibility

15. **Hacking, Ian.** *Representing and Intervening* (1983)
    - Relationship between representation and intervention
    - How institutional representations shape reality

## Cognitive Science and Reasoning

## Reading Strategy

For those new to these domains, suggested reading order:

**Phase 1: Foundations** (accessible introductions)
- Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow*
- Toulmin, *The Uses of Argument*
- Scott, *Seeing Like a State*
- Garrett, *Convicting the Innocent*
- Tufte, *Visual Display of Quantitative Information*

**Phase 2: Methodology** (research methods)
- Miles, Huberman & Saldaña, *Qualitative Data Analysis*
- King, Keohane & Verba, *Designing Social Inquiry*
- Pearl & Mackenzie, *The Book of Why*
- Creswell & Plano Clark, *Mixed Methods Research*

**Phase 3: Theory** (deeper theoretical foundations)
- Goldman, *Knowledge in a Social World*
- Vaughan, *The Challenger Launch Decision*
- Arendt, *The Human Condition*
- Foucault, *Discipline and Punish*
- Berger & Luckmann, *Social Construction of Reality*

**Phase 4: Specialization** (domain-specific deep dives)
- Select books relevant to your specific application domain
- Legal analysis: Laudan, Garrett, Roberts & Zuckerman
- Medical analysis: Groopman, Makary, Gawande
- Institutional analysis: DiMaggio & Powell, March & Simon, Reason
- Computational: Russell & Norvig, Jurafsky & Martin, Newman

**Phase 5: Advanced Topics** (cutting-edge theory and methods)
- Formal epistemology: Williamson, Sosa
- Advanced causation: Pearl, Morgan & Winship
- Advanced network analysis: Barabási, Watts, Newman
- Philosophy of science: Kuhn, Lakatos, Hacking
