## Data Protection Regulations

### GDPR (European Union)

**Scope**: Processing personal data of EU residents.

**Key Requirements**:
- **Legal Basis**: Consent, legitimate interest, legal obligation, etc.
- **Transparency**: Inform data subjects about processing
- **Data Subject Rights**: Access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection
- **Security**: Appropriate technical and organizational measures
- **Breach Notification**: Within 72 hours to supervisory authority
- **DPIA**: For high-risk processing
- **DPO**: Required for certain organizations

**Phronesis Compliance**:
- Local-first architecture (no data transfer to us)
- User controls all data subject rights
- When using AI: Review provider GDPR compliance

### UK GDPR (United Kingdom)

**Similar to EU GDPR** with UK-specific requirements.

**ICO Guidance**: Follow UK Information Commissioner's Office guidance.

### CCPA (California, USA)

**Scope**: Businesses processing California residents' personal information.

**Key Rights**:
- Right to know what personal information is collected
- Right to delete personal information
- Right to opt-out of sale
- Right to non-discrimination

**Phronesis Compliance**:
- No sale of personal information
- User controls all data
- Complete transparency

### Other Jurisdictions

Consult local counsel for:
- Canada (PIPEDA)
- Brazil (LGPD)
- Australia (Privacy Act)
- Other national/regional laws

## Health Information Regulations

### HIPAA (USA)

**Scope**: Protected Health Information (PHI) in healthcare contexts.

**Covered Entities**: Healthcare providers, health plans, clearinghouses.

**Business Associates**: Service providers handling PHI.

**Requirements**:
- **Privacy Rule**: Limit use and disclosure of PHI
- **Security Rule**: Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
- **Breach Notification**: Notify affected individuals and HHS
- **Business Associate Agreements**: Required contracts

**Phronesis Use with PHI**:
- Phronesis not a covered entity
- If you process PHI, you are responsible for compliance
- Implement appropriate safeguards:
  - Encryption at rest and in transit
  - Access controls
  - Audit logs
  - Secure disposal
- Consider impact of AI provider processing (BAA may be needed)

### Other Health Regulations

- **HITECH Act** (USA): Enhanced HIPAA enforcement
- **GDPR**: Health data is "special category" (extra protections)
- **Local health privacy laws**: Consult jurisdiction-specific counsel

## Legal Discovery Standards

### Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (USA)

**E-Discovery**: Electronic discovery rules (Rules 26, 34).

**ESI (Electronically Stored Information)**:
- Proportional to needs of case
- Reasonably accessible
- Native format or searchable format

**Phronesis in Discovery**:
- Can assist with discovery document analysis
- Export formats suitable for production
- Audit trails support authentication
- Ensure compliance with discovery obligations

### Preservation

**Duty to Preserve**: When litigation reasonably anticipated.

**Legal Hold**: Suspend routine deletion.

**Phronesis Cases**: If case materials subject to legal hold, ensure preservation.

## Expert Witness Standards

### Daubert Standard (USA Federal)

**Requirements**:
1. **Testability**: Can methodology be tested?
2. **Peer Review**: Has it been published and reviewed?
3. **Error Rate**: Known or potential error rates?
4. **Standards**: Standards controlling operation?
5. **Acceptance**: Generally accepted in relevant community?

**S.A.M. Designed for Daubert**:
- Systematic, documented methodology
- Validation framework
- Transparent process
- Subject to peer review

### Frye Standard (Some US States)

**General Acceptance**: Methodology must be generally accepted in relevant scientific community.

**S.A.M. Approach**: Building acceptance through publications, case studies, validation.

### Other Jurisdictions

Expert testimony standards vary. Consult local rules.

## Professional Conduct Rules

### Attorneys

**ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct**:
- Rule 1.1: Competence (including technology)
- Rule 1.6: Confidentiality
- Rule 3.3: Candor to tribunal
- Rule 3.4: Fairness to opposing party
- Rule 8.4: Misconduct

**State Variations**: Check state-specific rules.

**Phronesis Use**:
- Maintain competence in tool
- Ensure client confidentiality
- Verify findings before presenting
- Candor about methodology and limitations

### Journalists

**SPJ Code of Ethics**:
- Seek truth and report it
- Minimize harm
- Act independently
- Be accountable and transparent

**Phronesis Use**:
- Verify findings independently
- Protect confidential sources
- Minimize harm to non-public figures
- Transparent about methodology

### Researchers

**Research Ethics**:
- IRB approval for human subjects research
- Informed consent
- Data protection
- Research integrity
- Avoid plagiarism and fabrication

**Phronesis Use**:
- Obtain IRB approval where required
- Properly anonymize case studies
- Secure data storage
- Accurate reporting of findings
