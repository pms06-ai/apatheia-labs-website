The Problem

Why Institutions Fail Without Accountability

Institutional dysfunction persists because the evidence is fragmented, the patterns are hidden, and manual analysis cannot scale to document volumes that matter.

Claims Propagate Without Verification

A single unverified statement can be adopted by multiple agencies, accumulating apparent authority through repetition rather than evidence. By the time it reaches a decision-maker, the original uncertainty has been laundered into certainty.

Contradictions Hide in Volume

When case files span hundreds of documents, internal contradictions become invisible. A date mismatch on page 47 that undermines a conclusion on page 312 will never be caught by human review alone.

Selective Citation Goes Undetected

Reports that quote sources selectively—omitting context that contradicts their conclusions— appear authoritative. Without systematic source comparison, cherry-picking masquerades as thorough analysis.

Methodology

Systematic Adversarial Methodology

S.A.M. is the framework for reading institutional documents "against the grain"—treating every claim as requiring verification rather than acceptance.

Most document review assumes good faith: that claims are accurate, that sources are cited fairly, that timelines are consistent. S.A.M. inverts this assumption. It systematically tests every assertion, traces every claim to its origin, and maps how information flows between institutions.

1
ANCHOR

Identify false premise origin points—the moments where claims first emerge without adequate foundation, often from speculation presented as fact or hearsay treated as evidence.

"The assessment states X as fact, but the source document shows it was merely a hypothesis."
2
INHERIT

Track how claims propagate across institutional boundaries without verification. Map the transmission path from originator through each agency that adopts the claim.

"This statement appears in 6 subsequent reports, all citing each other rather than primary evidence."
3
COMPOUND

Document how authority accumulates through repetition. Identify where claims gain weight through endorsement rather than verification—authority laundering.

"The court accepted this as established fact because 4 professionals had stated it, though all traced to one unverified source."
4
ARRIVE

Map catastrophic outcomes back to originating false premises. Establish but-for causation: would the outcome have occurred if the anchor claim had been verified?

"The removal order was predicated on findings that trace directly to the unverified initial report."

CASCADE: Eight Contradiction Types

A taxonomy for classifying inconsistencies detected across document corpora

SELF

Internal Contradiction

A single document contradicts itself—different claims on different pages that cannot both be true.

INTER_DOC

Cross-Document Conflict

Two documents make incompatible claims about the same event, person, or fact.

TEMPORAL

Timeline Mismatch

Dates, sequences, or durations that are logically impossible given other established facts.

EVIDENTIARY

Evidence Gap

Claims presented as factual that lack supporting evidence or contradict available evidence.

MODALITY

Certainty Shift

A possibility becomes a probability becomes a certainty—without new evidence to justify the shift.

SELECTIVE

Cherry-Picking

Selective citation that omits context, qualifications, or contradicting passages from source material.

SCOPE

Scope Drift

Conclusions that exceed the scope of the analysis or apply findings beyond their valid domain.

UNEXPLAINED

Position Flip

A party changes position without explanation, or conclusions change without new evidence.
