Apatheia Labs Research Hub

Comprehensive research repository supporting the development of Phronesis FCIP and forensic intelligence methodologies. This hub organizes research across multiple disciplines to inform systematic analysis, evidence-based investigation, and institutional accountability frameworks.

Philosophy

“Clarity Without Distortion” requires deep understanding of how knowledge is constructed, validated, and distorted across domains. This research hub maintains a living collection of frameworks, methodologies, and empirical findings that:

  • Ground platform design in established professional practice
  • Inform analysis engine development with domain expertise
  • Provide theoretical foundations for systematic methodology
  • Document empirical evidence for cognitive biases and institutional failures
  • Enable interdisciplinary synthesis and pattern recognition

Current Research

Methodologies (Professional Investigation Frameworks)

Status: 6 comprehensive domain studies completed (January 2026)

  • Police Investigation Workflows - PEACE model, HOLMES2, chain of custody, CPIA disclosure
  • Investigative Journalism - ICIJ workflows, document pipeline, verification protocols
  • Legal eDiscovery - EDRM framework, TAR 2.0, timeline construction
  • Regulatory Investigations - GMC/HCPC/NMC standards, RCA framework
  • Intelligence Analysis - 66 SATs, ACH, Admiralty Code, bias mitigation
  • Academic Research Methods - PRISMA, Cochrane, qualitative analysis

Cross-Cutting Themes: - Evidence chain management and source reliability - Timeline construction and temporal analysis - Multi-document orchestration and contradiction detection - Quality control and peer review protocols - Bias mitigation through structured process - Defensible methodology and audit trails

Planned Research Areas

Cognitive Science & Neuroscience

  • Cognitive biases (confirmation, anchoring, availability, groupthink)
  • Memory reliability and eyewitness testimony
  • Decision-making under uncertainty
  • Pattern recognition and apophenia (false pattern detection)
  • Motivated reasoning and defensive processing
  • Neurological basis of belief formation and revision

Philosophy

  • Epistemology: Knowledge justification, skepticism, coherentism vs foundationalism
  • Logic: Formal reasoning, fallacies, argumentation theory
  • Philosophy of Science: Falsificationism (Popper), paradigm shifts (Kuhn), research programs (Lakatos)
  • Ethics: Professional conduct, moral reasoning frameworks
  • Philosophy of Language: Meaning, reference, vagueness, performative contradictions

Legal Studies

  • Evidence Law: Admissibility standards, hearsay rules, authentication, privilege
  • Administrative Law: Agency action, judicial review, procedural fairness
  • Constitutional Law: Due process, equal protection, fundamental rights
  • Tort Law: Negligence, causation, professional liability
  • Legal Theory: Legal realism, critical legal studies, law and economics

Political Science

  • Institutional Failure: Capture theory, principal-agent problems, regulatory failure
  • Accountability Mechanisms: Oversight bodies, transparency requirements, whistleblower protection
  • Public Choice Theory: Rational ignorance, concentrated benefits/diffuse costs
  • Bureaucratic Pathologies: Inertia, mission creep, accountability sinks
  • Power Analysis: Authority, legitimacy, compliance

Communication & Rhetoric

  • Propaganda Techniques: Bandwagon, loaded language, ad hominem, false dilemma
  • Framing Effects: Gain vs loss framing, metaphorical framing
  • Discourse Analysis: Power dynamics in language, presuppositions, implicature
  • Misinformation: Characteristics, spread patterns, correction strategies
  • Narrative Structure: Story arcs, narrative coherence, competing narratives

Data Science & Statistics

  • Causal Inference: DAGs, confounding, instrumental variables, difference-in-differences
  • Statistical Fallacies: P-hacking, base rate neglect, Simpson’s paradox, regression to mean
  • Machine Learning Ethics: Algorithmic bias, fairness metrics, interpretability
  • Network Analysis: Centrality measures, community detection, influence propagation
  • Time Series Analysis: Temporal patterns, structural breaks, forecasting

Sociology

  • Organizational Behavior: Groupthink, diffusion of responsibility, normalization of deviance
  • Social Mechanisms: Self-fulfilling prophecy, moral hazard, adverse selection
  • Power Structures: Hierarchies, status, authority, social capital
  • Institutional Theory: Isomorphism, legitimacy, organizational fields
  • Deviance: Labeling theory, moral panics, social control

Ethics & Professional Conduct

  • Medical Ethics: Beneficence, non-maleficence, autonomy, justice (four principles)
  • Research Ethics: Informed consent, privacy, dual use, conflicts of interest
  • Codes of Conduct: Professional standards across domains (medical, legal, psychological, engineering)
  • Moral Psychology: Moral foundations theory, moral disengagement, ethical fading
  • Applied Ethics: Trolley problems, double effect, acts vs omissions

Research Standards

Source Quality

  • Peer-reviewed academic research prioritized
  • Official standards and guidance documents (regulatory bodies, professional associations)
  • Case law and legal precedents
  • Practitioner handbooks and professional training materials
  • Investigative journalism from reputable outlets
  • Government reports and official inquiries

Documentation Requirements

Each research document should include: 1. Executive Summary - Key findings and takeaways 2. Methodology - How research was conducted 3. Core Concepts - Definitions and theoretical foundations 4. Empirical Evidence - Studies, data, case examples 5. Practical Applications - How findings inform platform design 6. Integration Points - Links to codebase and other research 7. Sources - Full citations with persistent URLs 8. Last Updated - Date and version tracking

Cross-Referencing

  • Explicit links between related research documents
  • Tags for discoverability (bias, evidence, temporal-analysis, etc.)
  • Interdisciplinary synthesis documents showing connections
  • Platform implementation notes (which engines, which features)

Contributing Research

Adding New Research

  1. Determine appropriate category (or create new if needed)
  2. Follow documentation standards template
  3. Add entry to INDEX.md with tags and summary
  4. Cross-reference related research
  5. Note integration points with codebase
  6. Update this README with new topic

Updating Existing Research

  • Maintain version history
  • Update “Last Updated” date
  • Note what changed and why
  • Preserve old versions if significant revisions
  • Update cross-references if scope changes

Research Requests

Integration with Phronesis FCIP

Direct Applications

S.A.M. Framework (8 Contradiction Types) Research foundation from investigation methodologies: - SELF/INTER_DOC: Journalism verification, Legal eDiscovery cross-doc analysis - TEMPORAL: Police chronologies, Legal timeline construction, Intelligence - EVIDENTIARY: Regulatory fact-finding, Journalism documentary > testimonial priority - MODALITY_SHIFT: Intelligence confidence levels, Academic reflexivity - SELECTIVE_CITATION: Journalism editorial review, Legal disclosure obligations - SCOPE_SHIFT: Regulatory ultra vires detection - UNEXPLAINED_CHANGE: Intelligence ACH, Journalism hypothesis testing

Analysis Engines Informed by domain-specific research: - Entity Engine → Legal entity extraction, Journalism network analysis - Temporal Engine → Police/Legal timelines, Intelligence chronologies - Contradiction Engine → All investigation domains, Logic/Philosophy - Omission Engine → Police reasonable lines of enquiry, Legal disclosure - Bias Engine → Intelligence SATs, Cognitive Science, Neuroscience - Professional Tracker → Regulatory standards, Ethics, Professional Conduct - Accountability Auditor → Political Science institutional failure, Sociology power structures - Expert Analyzer → Legal expert witness standards, Regulatory peer review - Narrative Analyzer → Communication/Rhetoric, Sociology narrative theory
